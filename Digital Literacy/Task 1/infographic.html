Digital Literacy is the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technologies — responsibly, safely, and effectively. It is not just about using devices; it is about thinking critically in a connected world and presenting yourself professionally online.

The Five Pillars of Digital Literacy

Core competencies every student must develop in the digital age

01

Digital Access

Equitable participation in the digital society — knowing where to find reliable tools and connectivity.

02

Information Literacy

Evaluating sources critically — identifying credible information and spotting misinformation.

03

Digital Communication

Communicating clearly and professionally across email, social media, and collaborative platforms.

04

Digital Safety

Protecting your data, privacy, and wellbeing — recognising threats like phishing and cyberbullying.

05

Digital Citizenship

Behaving ethically and responsibly online — respecting others and understanding digital rights.

Safe Internet Practices

Stay protected in your daily digital activity

Use strong, unique passwords — 12+ characters; use a password manager like Bitwarden.
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, banking, and academic portals.
Think before you click — verify email senders; do not open suspicious links or attachments.
Check for HTTPS — never enter personal data on sites without a secure padlock icon.
Keep software updated — patches fix security vulnerabilities on your device and apps.
Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks — use your college VPN when on open networks.

Professional Email Etiquette

Make the right impression with every message you send

To: professor.sharma@college.edu

Subject: Request for Assignment Extension – CS301 – [Your Name]

Dear Professor Sharma,

I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to request a two-day extension on the CS301 assignment due on 30 March, as I have been unwell this week. I have attached a medical note for your reference.

Thank you for your time and understanding.

Warm regards,
Priya Verma · Roll No. 2023BCS042 · CS301 – Section B

Do ✓

  • Use a clear, specific subject line
  • Address by title (Dr., Prof., Mr., Ms.)
  • Keep it concise and polite
  • Sign off with your full name & roll number

Don't ✗

  • Use casual greetings like "Hey"
  • Write in all caps or no punctuation
  • Reply all unnecessarily
  • Expect an instant response

Building a Professional Online Presence

Your digital footprint is your first impression to employers, collaborators, and the world

01

Audit Your Profiles

Google yourself. Review privacy settings on every social platform. Remove or archive content that does not represent you professionally.

02

Optimise LinkedIn

Use a clear headshot, a compelling headline, and an up-to-date summary. List projects, internships, and skills. Connect with peers and faculty.

03

Create a Portfolio

Showcase your best academic work on platforms like GitHub Pages, Behance, or a personal website. Keep it updated each semester.

04

Think Before You Post

Apply the "newspaper test" — would you be comfortable if this post appeared on the front page? Stay constructive and kind online.
